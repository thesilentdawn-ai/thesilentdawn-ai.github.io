Technology

What We Build

Veilglass systems encode operational intent into compact identifiers and deterministic verification logic. The goal is simple: make intent portable, auditable, and easy to validate across organizations and toolchains.

FIS-32 Intent-Validation Protocol

FIS-32 is a software-defined intent layer designed for high-volume environments such as event airspace. It supports issuing mission identifiers (cartouches), verifying them against a registry, and producing tamper-evident activity logs suitable for compliance and after-action review.

Key Capabilities

  • Compact cartouche identifiers: designed to fit constrained telemetry fields when needed.
  • Registry-backed validation: bind a declared intent to an authorized mission record.
  • Tamper-evident logging: hash-chained records to support audit and incident review.
  • Operational sorting: enable fast triage of authorized vs. anomalous tracks.

Where It Fits

  • Major event airspace management and temporary flight restrictions
  • Public safety UAS operations and mutual-aid coordination
  • Compliance workflows that require clear intent and traceable logs
  • Organizations that need interoperability without replacing hardware
